# Notes on Black Holes

- Black holes typically form when massive stars collapse at the end of their life cycle.
- After formation, black holes can grow by absorbing mass from their surroundings.
- The boundary around a black hole beyond which nothing can escape is called the event horizon.
- Black holes have extremely strong gravitational fields due to the concentration of mass in a very small area.
- There are different types of black holes, including stellar-mass black holes and supermassive black holes.
- Stellar-mass black holes form from the collapse of individual massive stars.
- Supermassive black holes are found at the centers of galaxies and have masses millions to billions of times that of our Sun.
- The existence of black holes is supported by both theoretical physics and astronomical observations.
- Black holes cannot be observed directly, but their presence is inferred through effects on nearby stars and gas.
- Recent advancements in technology, like the Event Horizon Telescope, have produced images of the shadow of a black hole, providing direct evidence of their existence.
